About FNATIC Valorant Sports Team

FNATIC are one of the longest serving organizations in professional esports, having been founded in 2004 in London, England. Their VALORANT division was established in February 2021 aftering signing SUMN FC. Fnatic are notoriously known for their dominance in Counter-Strike throughout their tenure, they have also picked up mutliple titled in League of Legends. Fnatic also compete in Dota 2, Halo, Rainbow Six: Seige, Apex Legends and FIFA.

FNATIC's Valorant team is admired for their teamwork, innovative strategies, and individual skill. They often engaged in exciting matches against other established esports organizations, making their games a highlight of the Valorant competitive scene.

"Our history is unparalleled. Founded in 2004, we are one of the most successful esports brands of all time, winning more tier 1 game tournaments than any other team globally. Winning is in our DNA. Today, driven by our ability to advance esports performance, our strong results, as well as a growing fanbase, we’re the world's third most-watched esports brand in 2020 - esports’ breakthrough year. Fast Company also deemed us 6th in the Most Innovative Gaming Companies of 2021.

It’s inevitable, then, that some of the world’s most forward-thinking brands - including AMD, BMW, Hisense, Monster and Jack Link's - choose to communicate to young people through Fnatic."

CEO - Sam Mathews

The CEO of Fnatic Esports, as of September 2021, is Sam Mathews. Sam Mathews is a prominent figure in the esports industry, known for co-founding Fnatic in 2004. Under his leadership, Fnatic has become one of the most successful and recognizable esports organizations globally. Mathews' vision and strategic decisions have played a crucial role in guiding the organization's growth, securing numerous championship titles across various competitive games, and expanding its reach through partnerships and sponsorships. His dedication to promoting esports on a global scale and his ability to adapt to the rapidly evolving industry landscape have solidified his reputation as a pioneering force in the esports realm.

Head Coach - Jacob Harris

Jacob "mini" Harris (born July 12, 1994) is a British coach for Fnatic. He is a former Counter-Strike: Source and Counter-Strike: Global Offensive player.

Mini is the English head coach of the Valorant team. Him duoing up with Boaster on SUMN FC made him one of the most respected coaches in Valorant. He also played a key role in the restructuring of the team and the new signings that have turned Fnatic into a top EU team again.

VCT LOCK//IN São Paulo 2023

On Saturday 4th March 2023, FNATIC were officially crowned the first ever Valorant LOCK//IN Champions after battling it out with the most elite teams from across the world. LOCK//IN is the highly anticipated global tournament that brings together all 32 top tier franchised teams from across the world to compete against each other in a single elimination bracket.

From February 13th until March 3rd, the 32 franchise teams were brought together from around the world to battle it out across three weeks of intense competition in São Paulo. Heading into the final, FNATIC went uncontested in their best-of-three and best-of-five match-ups, knocking out every single team it faced without ever losing a map (going 2-0 against Sentinels, 2-0 against FURIA, 2-0 against 100 Thieves and 3-0 against Natus Vincere). After dominating the bracket, and not losing a single map, Fnatic advanced to the Grand Final against home crowd favourites LOUD.

In a story that was written for the ages, and that will go down in Valorant Esports history, FNATIC continued their winning streak going 2-0 in the best-of-five match up. However, things got scary for the Black and Orange organisation after dropping the following two maps to make it 2-2 heading into one final decider.

In the final map, Icebox, the map that FNATIC’s team has become world famous for dominating, the team quickly fell round after round resulting in a 3-11 score which seemed impossible to turn around. After a tactical time out, FNATIC’s IGL Boaster turned around the momentum and switched the team back on, winning round after round in a historic comeback. After equalising at 11-11, FNATIC closed out the show with a 14-12 to win the trophy in a 3-2 triumph.
